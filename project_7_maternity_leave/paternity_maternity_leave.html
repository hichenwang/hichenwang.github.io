In the country with no mandatory paid maternity leave, there are companies caring more.

by Chen Wang  |  November 2, 2017

When I was pitching this story and mentioning there’s no maternity leave in the United States, my classmate, from a country other than America, burst out a “What?!”. And unfortunately, my clarification that “no PAID maternity leave, but you can get minimum 12 unpaid weeks” was followed by a big eye-rolling. Yes, you felt the disappointment from a foreigner, not to say the parents in this country.

But my clarification was still not accurate – actually, three states, California, New Jersey, and Rhode Island, currently offer paid family and medical leave, and New York will join them soon on Jan. 1, 2018. So next year, the number of the states offering paid family leave will rocket by 33.33%.

I tried several graph ideas to show America’s slightly embarrassing stance in this topic. Like a scatterplot in which every other OECD country fly in the sky while America staying at the origin point waiting for its moment, fists clenched.
